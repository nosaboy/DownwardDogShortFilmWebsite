00
Meet Martin

Have you ever wondered what goes on in the mind of a dog? What would they think about everyday? Or what they would say to you if they could speak? In Downward Dog, director Michael Killen opens a window to these hypothetical questions through Martin, a devoted and curious stay-at-home dog who ponders the mysteries of life. Through Martin’s eyes, we glimpse the beauty, humor, and sorrows of everyday life. Recently, Martin has become concerned with his beloved owner, Nan. Who has become distant due to her increasing workload. With a touch of whimsy and a dose of philosophy, Martin embarks on a journey of self-discovery, offering viewers an emotional experience that’s both introspective and heartwarming. Prepare to be swept into Martin’s world as he explores the essence of life with the sincerity only a dog could bring!

01
Friendship

Balancing friendship dynamics is often a delicate act. It is rooted in human nature that we instinctively assign value to those around us, shaping how we feel and respond to each relationship. In this chapter, Martin reflects on his struggles to find equilibrium with Nan. Although he cares deeply for her, he feels a lack of reciprocity that leaves him feeling undervalued. Martin sees himself as a “people pleaser,” often too hesitant to assert his needs or feelings. This tampers with his dynamic with Nan, especially when he feels Nan overlooks them. As he wrestles with this imbalance, he contemplates ways to capture her attention, hoping to restore a sense of mutual dependency in their relationship.

02
Walking

We’ve all experienced loneliness at some point. In this chapter, Martin opens up about his feelings of isolation. With Nan dedicating more time to her studies, their moments together have become sparse, leaving Martin feeling overlooked. When a close friend drifts away to follow a different path, it can sting, as if the connection you held dear was simply a passing interest to them. Martin wrestles with guilt for these thoughts, knowing everyone deserves to pursue their own passions, yet the ache of loneliness and the lingering sense of abandonment continue to deteriorate his mind as he navigates these complex emotions.

03
Aging

We often reflect on who we were and wonder about who we’ll become. We imagine what life might look like in a decade or recall the dreams we had when we were younger. In this chapter, Martin reflects on his younger self, a time when life felt uncomplicated. He longs for those days spent running freely along the shore, savoring the fresh ocean air. Now, at seven, he’s growing older; he notices the fatigue that now replaces the boundless energy he once had. Aging has crept up quietly, reminding him of the changes time brings for all of us.

04
Animal Instincts

Have you ever felt the urge to do something purely for your own satisfaction, even if it defies society’s standards? In this chapter, Martin delves into the thrill of rebellion. He dreams of breaking free from expectations, choosing to act on his own impulses rather than adhering to Nan’s rules. It’s a desire to shatter the glass that separates him from truly following his instincts. So, would you knock over that glass to embrace a moment of freedom?

05
Special Time

Every relationship has its cherished routines, moments that foster closeness. In this chapter, Martin observes that Nan seems to have lost interest in their morning "special time" together. But as he reflects, Martin begins to see Nan in a new light, realizing she may be more present and caring than he assumed. This shift fills him with gratitude, bringing a renewed sense of joy and relief. Sometimes, those who seem distant may care more deeply than we realize.

06
Driving

Everyone has a favorite pastime—whether it's singing, dancing, or creating art. For Martin, it’s the joy of riding in the car with Nan. In this chapter, he reflects on these drives and begins to question whether he’s been overanalyzing her every action. Realizing that life might be simpler than he once thought, Martin concludes that true happiness lies in embracing the present moment and enjoying what feels right. When we stop overcomplicating the small details, life opens up, inviting us to experience more joy in the here and now.

07
One Good Thing

In the final chapter, a major change reshapes Martin's life. Though initially seen as a setback, Martin chooses to view it positively, gaining newfound clarity about the trivial nature of life’s worries. This shift in perspective helps him let go of unnecessary stress. With Nan now spending more time with him, Martin finds contentment and peace, savoring the simple moments they share. As the chapter closes, he feels truly fulfilled, embracing life with a light heart and appreciating the beauty of living in the present.

In Downward Dog, director Michael Killen suggests that by letting go of unnecessary details and avoiding the overcomplication of relationships, one can experience a more emotionally fulfilling life.
